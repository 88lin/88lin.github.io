## Design Context

### Users
This site primarily serves Chinese-speaking visitors who want a fast, low-friction way to reach useful tools, media links, experiments, and small utility pages from one homepage. They may arrive on either mobile or desktop and usually want to find something quickly rather than browse a deep site structure.

### Brand Personality
Helpful, curious, approachable. The interface should feel friendly and organized, with a sense of discovery rather than a cold utilities dashboard. Emotionally it should balance trust, clarity, and a little delight.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a light editorial "digital garden" direction rather than a generic dark tech landing page. Favor warm paper-like neutrals, teal-green accents, soft depth, clear category structure, and Chinese-friendly typography. Avoid neon dark mode, overused startup gradients, and visually noisy card stacks.

### Design Principles
1. Findability comes first: users should understand where to click within seconds.
2. Organize before decorating: grouping, labels, and hierarchy matter more than effects.
3. Keep it warm and human: the site should feel curated, not machine-generated.
4. Use motion sparingly and purposefully: transitions should support clarity, not distract from navigation.
5. Mobile is a first-class experience: no critical content should be hidden on small screens.
